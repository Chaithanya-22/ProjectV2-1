Earth

Overview of Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known celestial body to support life. With a diameter of about 12,742 kilometers, it is the largest of the terrestrial planets. Composed mainly of rock and metal, Earth has a diverse landscape that includes mountains, valleys, oceans, and deserts. It orbits the Sun at an average distance of approximately 150 million kilometers, completing a revolution every 365.25 days, which defines our calendar year.

Atmosphere and Climate

Earth's atmosphere, composed of 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and trace amounts of other gases, plays a crucial role in sustaining life. It protects the planet from harmful solar radiation and helps regulate temperature. The climate varies across the globe, influenced by factors such as latitude, elevation, and ocean currents. This diversity creates a range of ecosystems, from tropical rainforests to arid deserts, each home to unique flora and fauna.
